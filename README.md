# Air Quality Data Analysis (AI)

## Overview
This project analyzes air quality data using AWS PartyRock's AI-powered data analysis feature. The goal is to identify pollution trends, understand variations over time, and extract meaningful environmental insights using generative AI.

---

## Dataset
The dataset contains air quality measurements from a monitoring station, including:

- PM10 (coarse particulate matter)
- PM2.5 (fine particulate matter)
- Carbon Monoxide (CO)
- Nitrogen Dioxide (NO₂)
- Ozone (O₃)
- Sulfur Dioxide (SO₂)

---

## Analysis Performed
- Identified key pollutants and their concentration levels  
- Analyzed trends and variations over time  
- Examined daily pollution patterns  
- Compared pollutant levels across different periods  
- Generated summary statistics using AI  

---

## Key Insights
- PM10 and PM2.5 are the dominant pollutants with high variability  
- Significant pollution spikes occur during mid and late periods  
- Daily patterns show higher PM10 levels in the afternoon and PM2.5 in early morning  
- Gaseous pollutants remain relatively low compared to particulate matter  

---

## Example Insight
- PM10 reached a peak of 106 µg/m³ indicating severe pollution levels  
- Pollution spikes were observed during late December  
- Daily variation shows higher pollution during afternoon and early morning hours
